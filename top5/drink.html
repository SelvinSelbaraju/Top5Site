Hydrate the Soul

To go along with the Top 5 Foods, you are also going to need something to drink! It’s amazing that there are so many different types of drinks for all sorts of ocassions.

From meeting friends in bars or chilling in coffee shops. Drinks bring people together in such special ways. They invoke all sorts of emotions within us, and I think that really is special.

The Top 5

Drumroll please...

5

Mojito

The only alcoholic drink on this list. This is one that I can come back to time and time again. It’s not super sweet and it’s so refreshing. They can pack a massive punch while not tasting like it, so be careful! The best one I’ve ever had was in Spain where it was absolutely massive.

4

Jasmine Tea

I don’t drink normal tea or coffee, and I don’t often drink Jasmine Tea. However, when I have Dim Sum, it is tradition to have Jasmine Tea. It tastes like hot water with a subtle, floral flavour. It’s great for washing down the oil in Dim Sum and it’s really warming for those colder days!

3

Homemade Orange Juice

You’ve probably had orange juice before, but have you ever made it yourself? Free from dilution and all the added nastiness. The pulp gives the drink a great consistency and it’s really easy to make. Perfect for a summer’s day!

2

Ice Milo

Milo was originally created in Australia, but I always have it when I go back to Malaysia. This ice chocolate drink is so refreshing in the Malaysian heat and the chocolate is so tasty. The ice makes it feel light and not too rich. I usually drink these with breakfast but they work at all points of the day.

1

Water

A boring top? I don’t think so. The vast majority of people do not drink enough water but it is just so good for you. Feeling hungry, tired or angry? Take a deep breath and have some water! It really works wonders. I can’t go anywhere without a water bottle. It really is life itself.
