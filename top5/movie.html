Lose Yourself in Cinema

In general, I prefer watching TV rather than films because films are a lot longer. However, every now and then I come across a film that makes me feel on so many levels. I am grateful that I have seen it.

At university, if there was a particular film I wanted to see, I would watch it in the cinema on a Monday and the tickets were super cheap. During COVID, I do really miss the cinema experience and am looking forward to going again when things open up.

The Top 5

Drumroll please...

5

Good Will Hunting

In this film, a university janitor is actually a genius mathematician, but he doesn’t believe it. At the same time, he has lots of personal troubles which a professor has to help manage. Watching it makes me wish I was better at Maths and it manages to make the watcher really feel for Will.

4

500 Days of Summer

This film is essentially a romcom with a twist. Instead of a happy ending, the hero explains how it all goes wrong. It’s definitely less light-hearted than your average romcom but it is clever, beautifully done and good to watch. It’s somewhat inspiring despite the unfortuante story.

3

The Shawnshank Redemption

Often regarded as one of the best films of all time and the top-rated film on IMDB. It follows the hero’s attempt to escape prison. He is abused yet he still pushes on. The story and sense of emotion it invokes in the watcher are unbelievable.

2

Avengers Endgame

To get the full benefit, you need to watch most of the films in the Marvel Cinematic Universe in order prior to watching this. This film ties everything up so perfectly and is so appealing for the fans of the MCU. The music, story and characters throughout the MCU are fantastic.

1

Forrest Gump

A classic, iconic film. It tells the story of Forrest Gump and the troubles of his life. Yet despite all of this, he overcomes his problems and achieves so many great things. The best thing is that he doesn’t even see it like that. It’s so inspiring, emotional and funny. A must watch.
